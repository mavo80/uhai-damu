Learn about blood banks, their importance, and how they operate to ensure safe blood supply in Kenya.

Kenya National Blood Transfusion Service (KNBTS)

KNBTS is mandated to ensure the availability of safe, sufficient, and quality-assured blood and blood products across Kenya. The service coordinates blood collection, testing, processing, storage, and distribution through a network of regional blood centres, satellite depots, and partner hospitals.

🔬 Blood Bank Operations

🩸

Collection

Blood is collected from voluntary donors through mobile drives and fixed donation centers following strict safety protocols.

🔬

Testing

Every donation undergoes rigorous testing for HIV, Hepatitis B & C, Syphilis, and other infectious diseases.

❄️

Processing

Blood is separated into components (red cells, platelets, plasma) to maximize the use of each donation.

📦

Storage

Components are stored at specific temperatures: Red cells at 2-6°C, Platelets at 20-24°C, Plasma frozen at -18°C.

📋 Blood Bank Standards & Regulations

  • All blood banks are licensed and regulated by KNBTS
  • Regular inspections and quality audits are conducted
  • Strict adherence to WHO blood safety standards
  • Compliance with Kenya's Health Act and Data Protection Act
  • Traceability from donor to patient (vein-to-vein tracking)
  • Continuous staff training and competency assessment

🚨 Emergency Blood Supply

In case of emergencies, blood banks maintain a 24/7 emergency response system. Hospitals can request blood through:

  • Emergency Hotline: +254 700 000 000
  • Online Request System: Available 24/7 on this platform
  • Direct Hospital Coordination: Regional blood centers

Response Time: Blood is dispatched within 30 minutes for critical emergencies.

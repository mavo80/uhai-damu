🩸 Why AB Positive is the Universal Receiver

AB positive blood contains both A and B antigens on the red blood cells, and also has the Rh factor. Because it has all three antigens, the immune system doesn't produce antibodies against any blood type.

💡 Why This Matters

🏥

Emergency Flexibility

AB+ patients can receive any blood type in emergencies when their exact type isn't available.

🩺

Plasma Donation

While AB+ can receive any red blood cells, they can only donate plasma to other AB+ patients.

🧪

Unique Immunology

AB+ individuals have no anti-A or anti-B antibodies, making them universal plasma recipients.
